vernacular systems design

    Ring#: 2
    Ring-date: Wed Jan 17, 2024

Vernacular systems design is the current title of an independent study I have been working on since summer 2020. During the Black Lives Matter movement I became interested in the alternative systems of care and safety being advocated for by prison abolitionists. I was specifically curious about what architectures could be rendered from the intangible infrastructures of embodied relations. From the q "how are systems translated spatially?", a breadth of inquiries emerged, which I titled "spatial systems design". I studied architecture as the primary spatial object because of the function of facades to mold our embodiment of systems. I presumed this a neutral function, but was not realistic about the logistical and systemic disenfranchisement of the public from spatial design.

I remained frustrated at a system cemented in a moment in time, and how slow progress is made because of it. But things do progress, dominant systems are designed to, and evidently outgrow the built environment at accelerating speed. I started to notice facades that bypass this disparity between rate of change in our physical environment and socio-economic systems, such as time, money, language. And the infrastructure that materialize their emergence? Information architecture. Unlike the physical environment, the public already has digital agency. The public are also already organizing in community at scale, generating knowledge and ideas that could inform sociotechnical systems. The consolidation of this knowledge is slow and often compromising, while social culture is rapidly emergent and nuanced. I'm curious about what kinds of informatic facades, can mould relational embodiments of autonomous/vernacular systems in the physical environment. Systems such as mutual aid and food systems.

Vernacular systems design refers to the capacity people have to emergently design and sustain systems, autonomously. VSD occurs beyond my semantic arrangement, and is unbound to medium. Given the aforementioned state of public agency however, my personal engagement with vsd will center information architecture, as I intend to design and research ways that digital interfaces can enable this proceedural capacity

Inquiries

    how are systems designed, mapped, modelled?
    how are different ontologies translated distinctly in the spatial map of a system?
    Are different types of information inherently classifiable as objectively sequential or objectively not-sequential?
    how do autonomy and collective coexist in a systems design process? in the interface faciltating/modelling that process? logistical delegation of technical and domain capacities, decision making, consensus processes, proceedural and interface mechanisms for emergent adaptation?
