article title

vernacular systems design

    Ring#:1
    Ring-date: Nov 13, 2023

Vernacular systems design is the current title of an independent study I have been working on since the summer of 2020. During the Black Lives Matter movement I became interested in the alternative systems of care and safety being advocated for by prison abolitionists. I was specifically curious about what architectures could be rendered from the intangible infrastructures of embodied relations. From the question "how are systems translated spatially?" emerged a breadth of inquiries which I titled "spatial systems design". I studied architecture as the primary spatial object because of the function of facades to mold our embodiment of systems. I presumed this to be a neutral capacity, equally effective for oppression as for liberation, failing to recognize this as the precise reason for disenfranchising inhabitants from spatial planning. This past summer (2023) I shifted my work, and its title, to reflect this realization.

The replacement of "spatial" with "vernacular" emancipates method from medium, and emphasizes the (significance) of context in design process and product. Systems design inherits connotations of agency and locale from vernacular, which is defined as a dialect emerging from a people in place. It also retains a spatial connotation, given the extended term "vernacular architecture", the dialect infrastructure emerged by a people in place.
